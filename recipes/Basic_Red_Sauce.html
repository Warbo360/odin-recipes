Odin's Basic Red Sauce Recipe

Description

This sauce while simple in prep and ingredients given enough time will turn any pasta dish into a classic friends and family will end up loving. This is best served with any pasta that will can cling sauce onto it well, our recommendations being classic spaghetti, penne, or something like a rigatoni. If you can find bronze die-pulled pasta all the better as that gives the pasta a rougher texture which is better for sauce to cling onto.

While the sauce is basic, you can customize it however suits your needs as well, adding whatever protein you have lying around or topping a protein with the sauce as well (looking at you chicken parm) is a great way to get additional uses out of this recipe.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium to large yellow onion
  • 2 large cans of San Marzano or plum style tomatoes with skins peeled
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste
  • 2 cloves of garlic, more if desired
  • Enough olive oil to coat the onions and bottom of the stockpot
  • Whole sprig or two of fresh basil, save some for garnish
  • 1-2 tablespoons of oregano, fresh or dried is fine
  • 1-2 tablespoons of parsely, fresh or dried is fine
  • Choice of protein if desired, for a thick sauce add atleast a pound
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper to season-to-taste at the end

Steps

  1. Finely dice onion and add to stock pot with plenty of olive oil, cook on medium-high heat unitl onions are cooked all the way through but not browned
  2. Brown choice of protein if desired while the onion also cooks
  3. Add the tomato paste to the pot and cook 1-2 minutes, meanwhile crush garlic with a garlic press
  4. Add garlic to the onion and tomato paste combination, as soon as fragrant add the cans of tomatoes, fill one of the cans with water and also add to the pot
  5. Add protein if sufficiently browned at this point with seasonings, saving the salt and pepper for end of the cooking process
  6. Bring the sacue to a simmer and let that cook unit the sauce thickens enough to coat the back of a spoon where if you swipe your finger across the back it leaves a distinct streak, stir every 10-15 minutes while cooking
  7. Once sauce has thickened to desired thickness turn the heat off and top sauce on cooked pasta or protein of choice garnishing with fresh basil if desired
